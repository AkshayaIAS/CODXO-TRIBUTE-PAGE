Kalpana Chawla

1962-2003

"The Journey matters as much as the goal"

Biography

Kalpana Chawla, an aerospace engineer and the first woman of Indian origin in space, has left an indelible mark on the world through her remarkable achievements and enduring legacy. Born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, Haryana, India, she transformed her childhood dreams of reaching the stars into reality, inspiring millions along the way.

Early Life and Education

Kalpana Chawla's journey began in a small town in India where she displayed an early passion for aviation and space. She attended Tagore School in Karnal and later pursued a Bachelor of Engineering degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Punjab Engineering College in Chandigarh. Her academic prowess and determination led her to the United States, where she earned a Master of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington in 1984, followed by a Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Colorado Boulder in 1988.

NASA Career and Space Missions

Kalpana Chawla joined NASA in 1994, where her dream of space exploration took flight. She was selected as an astronaut candidate in 1994 and made her first journey into space aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997. As a mission specialist and primary robotic arm operator, she contributed to the successful deployment of the Spartan Satellite.


Her second space mission came in 2003 on the Space Shuttle Columbia STS-107. Tragically, the mission ended in disaster when the shuttle disintegrated upon re-entry into Earth's atmosphere on February 1, 2003, resulting in the loss of all seven crew members. Despite this devastating event, Kalpana Chawla's courage and dedication continue to inspire future generations.

Legacy and Honors

Kalpana Chawla's legacy is one of perseverance, excellence, and inspiration. She received numerous posthumous honors, including:


△Congressional Space Medal of Honor:One of NASA's highest honors, awarded for extraordinary accomplishments in space.


△NASA Distinguished Service Medal:Recognizing her significant contributions to the space program.


△NASA Space Flight Medal: Honouring her participation in space missions.
